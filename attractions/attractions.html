Valencia, a progressive municipality in Negros Oriental, rises proudly in the island’s mountainous heartlands, where beaches may be absent but nature and culture abound in unforgettable ways. Surrounded by lush greenery and cool highland air, the town reveals hidden treasures at every turn—from majestic waterfalls and eco‑adventure parks to fascinating educational museums that showcase its rich heritage. Known as “The Little Baguio of Negros”, Valencia offers more than you can imagine, blending scenic beauty, history, and adventure into a destination that promises thrills, serenity, and discovery like no other.

TIERRA ALTA RESIDENTIAL RESORT

Tierra Alta Residential Resort in Palinpinon, Valencia, Negros Oriental is the perfect alternative for those seeking both luxury and tranquility, perched 800 feet above sea level with breathtaking views that capture the best of both worlds—the sparkling sea and neighboring islands of Cebu and Siquijor on one side, and the lush greenery of Valencia’s mountains on the other. Just a quick 12‑minute drive to Dumaguete City, it offers the rare balance of a serene suburban lifestyle with the convenience of nearby urban living. With its scenic location, refreshing mountain air, and panoramic landscapes, Tierra Alta is more than a resort—it’s a picture‑perfect escape that blends relaxation, sophistication, and accessibility in one unforgettable destination.

MANGO RANCH

In Valencia, Mango Ranch offers a one‑of‑a‑kind destination that blends rustic elegance, adventure, and leisure. Its centerpiece, The Barn at Mango Ranch, features 1,700 sqm of indoor space for up to 500 guests, complete with a Bridal Suite, Caterer’s Area, Supplier’s Lounge, ample parking, and a grand staircase for stunning photo moments. Outside, an 8,000 sqm landscaped lawn sets the stage for garden weddings, concerts, cocktail events, or even dramatic arrivals via helipad, enhanced by natural daylight and romantic evening lighting. The sprawling Mango Ranch Waterpark, the first of its kind in Negros Oriental, spans eight hectares of adrenaline rides, refreshing pools, and serene escapes for families, friends, and solo adventurers. Completing the experience are grazing animals, authentic steak dining, a bar for alcoholic beverages, and the iconic purple Mango Ranch Windmill, making every visit truly unforgettable.

FOREST CAMP

Just minutes away from Valencia’s town proper lies the enchanting Forest Camp, a highland haven where crystal‑clear waters rush straight from the mountains and invite visitors into a refreshing escape. For only ₱200, guests can step into this natural paradise surrounded by lush greenery, with pools brimming with cold spring water, free tables and chairs scattered across the grounds, and cozy tents or cottages available for groups. Adventure seekers can add excitement with ziplining and wall climbing, while those craving serenity can enjoy swimming, forest views, or even extend their stay with night dips and overnight accommodations. Accessible by tricycle or habal‑habal in just a few minutes, Forest Camp is the perfect blend of relaxation, thrill, and the cool mountain charm that makes Valencia unforgettable.

PULANGBATO FALLS

The Pulangbato Falls of Valencia, whose name means “red rock” in the local dialect, is a natural wonder where a striking cascade flows into a basin surrounded by crimson stones, earning it the title of the “red princess.” While scientists have yet to determine the cause of the rocks’ unusual coloring, the waters remain clean, safe, and refreshingly cold. Developed by the Tourism Office of Valencia, the site continues to undergo improvements, offering chairs, tables, and sunbathing lounges for visitor comfort. Although the falls are geographically closer to Dumaguete City, most travelers begin their trip from the Valencia town plaza for convenience in exploring other tourist spots. Much like Forest Camp, Pulangbato features invigorating waters complemented by two manmade pools suitable for both children and adults, along with a small snack bar serving beverages, desserts, and simple meals—making it an ideal destination for a cool dip and a memorable eco‑tourism experience in Valencia.

RED ROCK HOT SPRING

After enjoying a refreshing dip at Pulangbato Falls, continue your journey just 850 meters downhill to discover the soothing embrace of the Red Rock Hot Spring. This natural wonder is the perfect spot to ease tired muscles, rejuvenate the body, and refresh the skin, all while being surrounded by the striking beauty of reddish rocks that give the spring its name. The warm waters blend harmoniously with the scenic backdrop of rolling hills and lush trees, creating a tranquil atmosphere where relaxation and nature meet. Whether you come for healing, leisure, or simply to bask in the unique landscape, the Red Rock Hot Spring offers an unforgettable highland retreat in Valencia.

CASARORO FALLS

The majestic Casaroro Falls in Valencia is a destination that both challenges and rewards its visitors, earning praise for its towering beauty while testing endurance with over 350 concrete steps and a rugged trek through boulders and flowing waters before reaching its tranquil cascade. Unlike other tourist spots in the municipality, Casaroro offers a more raw and untamed experience, with no tables or chairs at the main grounds—only comfort rooms along the way and cement blocks that serve as makeshift seating—making it wise to bring foldable chairs and towels for convenience. Though the demanding hike may discourage some, the sight of the falls instantly washes away fatigue, offering breathtaking scenery and the perfect photo opportunity that makes every step of the journey worthwhile.

MONKEY SANCTUARY

The Monkey Sanctuary of Valencia, Negros Oriental, located along Palinpinon Street in Barangay Buayahan, is a unique eco‑tourism destination where visitors can enjoy the rare experience of monkey viewing in their natural habitat. Surrounded by lush greenery and the refreshing highland air of Valencia, the sanctuary offers a peaceful environment where these playful creatures roam freely, providing both entertainment and a deeper appreciation of wildlife. It has become a favorite stop for families, friends, and travelers who wish to witness the monkeys up close, take memorable photographs, and learn about the importance of protecting local biodiversity. More than just a tourist attraction, the Monkey Sanctuary also serves as a reminder of the harmonious coexistence between humans and nature, making it a must‑visit spot for anyone exploring Valencia’s rich blend of adventure and ecological wonders.

SULFUR VENTS IN PULANGBATO

Sulfur Mountain in Pulangbato, Valencia, Negros Oriental is a fascinating natural attraction, famous for the constant steam rising from its surface that creates a misty, almost otherworldly effect perfect for photographs. While the fog‑like steam may look enchanting, it carries the distinct smell of rotten eggs due to the area’s high sulfur content, a reminder of the geothermal activity linked to the potentially active Mt. Talinis. Visitors should note that those with breathing or lung conditions are advised to avoid prolonged exposure, as the fumes can be harsh. The site itself features striking reddish rocks, giving Pulangbato its name (“red rock”), colored by sulfur deposits that add to its dramatic landscape. Often paired with a trip to the nearby Pulangbato Falls, the Sulfur Vents offer a unique glimpse into nature’s raw power, blending scenic beauty with the awe of volcanic energy.

CHOSEN'S CAFE

Chosen’s Café is a cozy highland retreat tucked away in the scenic mountains of Valencia, Dumaguete. More than just a café, it is a sanctuary where guests can pause, breathe, and reconnect with the beauty of nature. Perched above the city, the café offers sweeping views of rolling hills, lush greenery, and the distant coastline, creating the perfect backdrop for relaxation and reflection. The cool mountain air refreshes the senses, while the gentle ambiance invites you to linger a little longer over a warm cup of coffee or a hearty homemade meal.

VALENCIA PLAZA

Valencia Plaza is the perfect destination for those who prefer leisure and recreation over swimming or hiking. At the heart of the town, the plaza offers a lively playground, a basketball court, and a tennis court for visitors who enjoy being physically active. For those who simply want to relax, the plaza is surrounded by vibrant flowers and lush plants that create a refreshing atmosphere. As the day winds down, the plaza becomes even more inviting with the aroma of the town’s famous crispy and savory lechon belly, a must‑try delicacy that adds flavor to the Valencia experience.
